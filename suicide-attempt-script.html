Script for Responding to Critical Self-Harm/Suicide Attempt Incidents

Begin with:
“I’m here with you, I’m going to call for some help”

If the student requires urgent medical care:

  • Call 111 immediately for emergencies (e.g. uncontrolled bleeding, loss of consciousness).
    If 111 is called, Director of Boarding must then be called immediately. You may need to ask someone else to assist you in calling the Director of Boarding as you will likely need to stay on the phone with the police dispatcher.

Call to Director of Boarding:

Communicate the following in a calm, neutral tone:
  • Level of risk, e.g., “This is a critical incident requiring urgent support”
  • Student’s name
  • Location
  • Whether a call to 111 has been made

Whilst waiting for support to arrive:

  • Reassure the student that you’re there with them and that help is on the way.
  • If the student is talking, listen without judgement.
  • If the student is worrying about what will happen next (e.g., parents finding out), say things like:
    “I know you’re worried, our priority right now is keeping you safe”.
  • Do NOT promise to keep anything secret.
  • Try to encourage the student to breathe with you, and continue to provide reassurance that you’re there and help is coming.
  • If the student is talking about what has happened, validate their feelings without weighing in on their actions, e.g.:
    “I’m sorry to hear things have been so hard for you”
    “That sounds like it’s been really awful”
    “I’m sorry you’ve been going through that”
    “I’m sorry you’re feeling like that”

Note: This script is a guideline for immediate response and should be used alongside your comprehensive school crisis plan and relevant training.
